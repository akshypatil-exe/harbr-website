GTA 6 PC Confirmation: Rockstar Schedules Late 2026 Release and Outlines Tech Specs

By Gaming Trends Desk • July 13, 2026 • 5 min read

Following years of intense speculation, Rockstar Games has officially confirmed that **Grand Theft Auto VI** will launch on the PC platform in **Late 2026**. Alongside the announcement, developers released an initial breakdown of PC-specific graphical features and hardware design systems that are set to redefine sandbox graphics.

Ray Reconstruction and AI NPC Behaviors

While console players have been enjoying the game, the PC version is engineered to take advantage of the latest GPU hardware. Rockstar revealed that GTA 6 on PC will natively support real-time Ray Reconstruction. This technology removes traditional denoising lag, generating highly accurate reflections and light bounces off wet pavements and neon-soaked windows. Additionally, the density of AI-driven pedestrian and vehicle traffic will scale dynamically with high-core-count processors.

Hardware Spec Targets

Rockstar’s engine architects have built the game to scale. Minimum specs require an SSD and at least 16GB of system memory, while the recommended specifications suggest utilizing GPUs equipped with hardware-based AI frame-generation technologies to achieve steady frame rates at 4K resolution. The PC build will also support ultra-wide monitors and multi-monitor configurations out-of-the-box.

Mod Support Policies

An open question has always been Rockstar's stance on community modifications. The announcement explicitly mentions plans to support custom roleplay community servers through official integrations. However, the developers warned that third-party script injectors modifying online sessions will face aggressive automated security bans to protect the multiplayer ecosystem from hacking exploits.
