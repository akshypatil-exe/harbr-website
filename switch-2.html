Nintendo Switch 2: Specs, Price, and Launch Title Rumors

By Tech Trends Desk • July 10, 2026 • 6 min read

Speculation surrounding Nintendo's next-generation hybrid console is reaching a fever pitch. With shipping manifests, developer kit leaks, and supply chain reports aligning, we compile the most credible specifications, pricing targets, and software lineup rumors for the Nintendo Switch 2.

The Nvidia Chipset Upgrade

According to shipping records, the Switch 2 will utilize a custom Nvidia system-on-chip codenamed T239. This chip features an Ampere-based GPU containing 1536 CUDA cores. Crucially, the custom hardware supports Nvidia's DLSS (Deep Learning Super Sampling) upscaling tech, allowing the handheld to output 4K resolution when docked while maintaining a high frame rate.

Display & Design Adjustments

Reports suggest the baseline model will launch with an 8-inch LCD screen rather than OLED to manage manufacturing costs. Storage is expected to get a boost to 256GB of fast flash memory, addressing the storage limitations of the original console. The signature Joy-Con controllers are also rumored to use magnetic sliders instead of rails to prevent drift.

Launch Window & Launch Lineup

A pricing target of $399 is widely expected, positioning the console competitively against current consoles. As for launch software, rumors suggest a new 3D Mario title and an optimized next-gen version of current generation hits will showcase the hardware's updated processing power.
